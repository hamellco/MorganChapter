Lewis Henry Morgan Chapter of NYSAA

The Lewis Henry Morgan Chapter of the New York State Archaeological Association (NYSAA), founded in 1916 and named after the renowned anthropologist Lewis Henry Morgan, is the oldest and largest chapter dedicated to fostering an interest in New York State archaeology. Morgan’s significant contributions, including “League of the Iroquois” and “Ancient Society,” have earned international recognition. Our chapter, based in Rochester, meets almost monthly to promote archaeological enthusiasm and historical exploration, welcoming members from diverse backgrounds to share in our passion for New York archaeology.

Presentation: Photographing Small Artifacts by Larry Naylor

In this talk, Larry Naylor will discuss strategies for taking photographs of small archaeological artifacts for presentations, reports, and publication using digital photography and Photoshop for processing images. Lighting techniques, positioning, backgrounds, and scales are important for accurate documentation of artifacts. Photographing lithics, ceramics, glass, coins, metal objects, and skeletal material will be demonstrated throughout the presentation.
